  "summary": "The Kamba (Akamba) are a Bantu ethnic group native to the lower eastern counties of Kenya (Ukambani: Machakos, Kitui, Makueni). They are Kenya's fifth-largest ethnicity (4.66M, ~9.8% of national population, 2019 census). Historically renowned as long-distance traders, the Kamba controlled trade routes from the Indian Ocean to Lake Victoria and Lake Turkana, acting as middlemen between the coast and interior. They are closely related linguistically and culturally to the Kikuyu, Embu, Mbeere, and Meru, forming the GEMA (Gikuyu-Embu-Meru-Akamba) cluster. The Kamba are distinguished by their wood carving artistry, their role as the 'premier martial race' of British colonial forces (King's African Rifles), and a unique diaspora population in Paraguay (Kamba Cuá) descended from enslaved soldiers of General Artigas (1820). Traditional religion centers on Mulungu/Ngai with intercessory spirits (Aimu); the Kilumi rain-making dance remains a significant cultural practice. The community produced prominent anti-colonial figures (Syokimau, Muindi Mbingu, Paul Ngei) and contemporary political leaders (Kalonzo Musyoka, Charity Ngilu, Alfred Mutua).",
