  "summary": "The Somali people are a Cushitic ethnic group native to the Somali Peninsula (Horn of Africa), forming one of the largest ethnic groups on the continent by landmass coverage. Numbering approximately 17.6 million in Somalia, 5.7 million in Somaliland, 4.6 million in Ethiopia, 2.8 million in Kenya, and 586,000 in Djibouti, with a global diaspora exceeding 2 million. Somalis are predominantly Sunni Muslim (Shafi'i school) and speak the Somali language (Af-Soomaali), a Lowland East Cushitic language of the Afroasiatic family. Genetic and archaeological evidence indicates indigenous habitation of the Horn for over 7,000 years, with the ancient Land of Punt forming a key component of Somali ethnogenesis. Classical antiquity sources reference the Macrobians and the Barbar/Barbaria coast. Islam was introduced in the 7th century, with Somalis among the earliest non-Arab converts. Medieval Somali sultanates (Ifat, Adal, Ajuran, Geledi, Majeerteen, Hobyo) dominated regional trade and hydraulic engineering, with Ahmed Gurey's Adal Sultanate being the first African polity to employ cannon warfare. The 20th century witnessed anti-colonial resistance (Dervish movement, 1899-1920) led by Mohammed Abdullah Hassan, followed by independence and unification in 1960. Somali society is organized through a patrilineal clan system comprising five principal clans: Darod, Isaaq, Dir, Hawiye, and Rahanweyn, with a sophisticated customary law (xeer) operating parallel to Sharia. Somali culture is renowned for its oral poetry ('nation of poets'), maritime trade heritage, frankincense and myrrh production, and distinctive architecture ranging from ancient taalo pyramids to medieval coral-stone cities. The Somali flag (1954) features the Star of Unity, representing the five territories of Greater Somalia. The civil war (1991-present) precipitated a global diaspora, establishing significant Somali communities in North America, Europe, the Middle East, and Oceania.",
