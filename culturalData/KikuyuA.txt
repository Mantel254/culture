# Kikuyu People

## Basic Information
**Ethnic Group**: Kikuyu (Agĩkũyũ)
**Total Population**: Approximately 8.1 million (8,148,668 as of 2019 census)
**Percentage of Kenya's Population**: ~17.13%
**Status**: Largest ethnic group in Kenya
**Related Terms**:
- Person: Mũgĩkũyũ
- People: Agĩkũyũ
- Language: Gĩkũyũ
- Country: Bũrũrĩ Wa Gĩkũyũ

## Classification and Origin
**Ethnic Classification**: Northeastern-Bantu ethnic group
**Origin**: West-Central Africa (modern-day Cameroon/Nigeria region)
**Migration History**: Part of the Thagicu group that migrated through the Congo Basin and settled around Mount Kenya
**Genetic Composition**: Approximately 20-25% Nilotic ancestry and 32-36% Cushitic ancestry due to geographic interactions
**Settlement Timeline**: First reached northern slopes of Mount Kenya around 3rd century CE, established cultural core in Murang'a by 13th century

## Geographic Distribution
**Primary Location**: Fertile central highlands of Kenya
**Regions with Significant Populations**: Central Kenya, especially around Mount Kenya
**Closely Related Communities**: Embu, Meru, Akamba (due to intermarriage before colonization)
**Cultural Heartland**: Murang'a county is considered the cradle of the Kikuyu people

## Language
**Native Language**: Gĩkũyũ language (Bantu language family)
**Other Languages**: Swahili and English as lingua francas
**Writing System**: Uses modified Roman alphabet introduced by missionaries
**Language Note**: The term "Kikuyu" is the Swahili borrowing of the autonym "Gĩkũyũ"

## Social and Political Structure
**Traditional Organization**: Nine clans (mĩhĩrĩga)
**Clan Names**: Anjirũ, Agacikũ, Ambũi, Angũi (Aithiegeni), Angechi (Aithĩrandũ), Aacera, Ambura (Aakĩũrũ or Eethaga), Airimũ (Agathiigia), Angarĩ (Aithekahuno) and Aicakamũyũ
**Clan System**: Matrilineal descent - each clan traces lineage to a single female ancestor, daughter of Mumbi
**Political Power**: Exercised by ruling council of elders for each clan, with apex council for the whole community
**Leadership**: Overall council led by a headman (nation's spokesman)

## Traditional Religion and Spirituality
**Belief System**: Animism with veneration of ancestors and spiritual entities
**High God**: Ngai (also known as Mwene-Nyaga, Mũrungu, Mũlungu)
**Sacred Sites**: Worship under sycamore tree (Mũkũyũ) or fig tree (Mũgumo)
**Sacred Tree for Women**: Olive tree (Mũtamaiyũ)
**Mount Kenya**: Central spiritual significance as site of divine manifestation
**Philosophical Principles**:
1. Veneration of ancestors
2. Veneration of nature and nature deities
3. Veneration of lower and higher gods
4. Veneration of a distant High God
5. Belief in afterlife with possibility of reincarnation

## Timekeeping and Generational System
**Time Documentation**: Through initiation sets (riika)
**Seasons**:
1. Mbura ya njahĩ (big rain season): March to July
2. Magetha ma njahĩ (black bean harvest): July to early October
3. Mbura ya Mwere (short rain season): October to January
4. Magetha ma Mwere (millet harvesting season)
5. Mbura ya Kĩmera

**Initiations**:
- Metumi (Mũrang'a): Boys initiated annually for 9 years, then 4.5-year break (mũhingo)
- Gaki (Nyeri): Annual initiations for 4 years, then 9-year mũhingo
- Girls initiated annually (less documented)

**Ruling Generations (Itwĩka)**: Lasted ~35 years each
- Manjiri (1512-1546)
- Mamba (1547-1581)
- Tene (1582-1616)
- Aagu (1617-1651)
- Manduti (1652-1686)
- Cuma (1687-1721)
- Ciira (1722-1756)
- Mathathi (1757-1791)
- Ndemi (1792-1826)
- Iregi (1827-1861)
- Maina (1862-1897)
- Mwangj (1898 onward)

**Ituĩka Ceremony**: Transfer of power between generations (last one 1898-1899, next scheduled 1925-1928 but thwarted by colonial government)

## Colonial Period (1888-1945)
**First Contact**: 1888 with British explorers
**Early Conflict**: Waiyaki Wa Hinga burned down Lugard's fort in 1890, was buried alive in 1892
**Colonial Administration**: British East Africa Company, then direct Crown rule through East African Protectorate (1895)
**Land Disputes**: White settlers given fertile highlands from 1902, leading to conflicts
**World War I**: Kenya served as British base, Carrier Corps mobilized over 400,000 Africans
**Political Organizations**:
- Young Kikuyu Association/East African Association (1921, Harry Thuku)
- Kikuyu Central Association (KCA, 1924), with Jomo Kenyatta as Secretary General
**World War II**: Kikuyu served in King's African Rifles, war stimulated African nationalism

## Independence Movement (1945-1963)
**Political Evolution**:
- Kenya African Study Union (KASU, 1944, multi-ethnic)
- Kenya African Union (KAU, 1946, nationalist organization)
**Mau Mau Uprising (1952-1956)**: Armed revolt led by Dedan Kimathi for complete independence
**Independence**: Kenya gained independence from British colonial rule in December 1963

## Culture
**Literature**:
- Pre-colonial: Oral folklore only
- Post-missionary: Written literature using modified Roman alphabet
- Notable authors: Ngũgĩ wa Thiong'o, Meja Mwangi
- Ngũgĩ's works include "Caitaani mũtharaba-Inĩ" (Devil on the Cross), "Matigari", "Murogi wa Kagogo" (Wizard of the Crow)

**Music**:
- Traditional forms: Kibaata, Nduumo, Muthunguci
- Traditional instrument: Mũtũrĩrũ (oblique bark flute)
- Modern: Vigorous recording industry for secular and gospel music
- Musical scale: Pentatonic

**Cinema**: Popular only in 21st century, includes comedies like Machang'i series and Kihenjo series

**Cuisine**:
- Staples: Yams, sweet potatoes, Gītheri (maize and beans)
- Dishes: Mūkimo (mashed green peas and potatoes), Kīmitū (mashed beans and potatoes), Irio (mashed dry beans, corn and potatoes)
- Meat: Mūtura (goat sausage), roast goat, beef, chicken
- Porridge: Ūcūrū (fermented porridge from corn, millet or sorghum flour)
- Vegetables: Collards, spinach, carrots

## Modern Religious Affiliation
**Majority**: Christians of various denominations
**Minority Religions**: Islam, Traditional African Religion, Judaism
**Judaism Practice**: ~60 people in Kusuku practice normative Judaism (similar to Conservative Judaism)
**Religious-Cultural Conflict**: Presbyterian Church of East Africa prohibited members from Kikuyu cultural rite "Mburi cia Kiama" in 2018, causing disturbances
